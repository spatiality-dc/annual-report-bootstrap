Today’s Problems, Yesterday’s Toolkit

This report, produced in partnership with the NYU Governance Lab is an assessment of innovation in the Australian public service. The report was co-authored by Gov Lab’s Professor Beth Noveck and MSDI’s Professor Rod Glover. It was developed for the Australia and New Zealand School of Government (ANZSOG). It outlines the results of a world-first survey of nearly 400 public servants and found that while Australia’s public sector is generally well-functioning, it faces a ‘creeping crisis’ of effectiveness and legitimacy. The authors propose three areas of focus moving forward: develop a 21st century toolkit for public problem solving, design more effective skills training, and encourage institutional experimentation.

Climate Risk and the Financial System

Australia can learn from a new wave of global climate policy to create sustainable finance, according to a report released in 2019 by MSDI. The report was co-authored by Chris Barrett, Executive Director, Finance Strategy and the European Climate Foundation, and Anna Skarbek, CEO of our ClimateWorks Australia team. The paper surveys the policy and politics of emerging international debate about climate risk for the finance system. It also assesses implications for Australia and frames some key questions for Australian policymakers to respond to them. The paper argues that Australia has numerous positive reform examples to draw on and that the ingredients are there for Australia to make significant progress, and quickly.

From Townsville to Tuvalu

In 2019, MSDI and Monash Medicine partnered with the Global Health Alliance (GHA) to release a report on health and climate change in Australia and the Asia Pacific Region. The report makes an important contribution to our understanding about the way in which climate change is already affecting human health and on potential future impacts. It makes proposals for how Australian Governments might respond to the challenge, arguing that Australia’s aid, health and agricultural portfolios have the opportunity to develop policies that build resilience in our region to the impacts of climate change on human health.

Social Inclusion Index

MSDI’s BehaviourWorks researchers developed Australia’s first social inclusion index in 2019. Commissioned as part of our work with Inclusive Australia, the report holistically measured Australia’s progress towards social inclusion. Our research showed that nearly one in four Australians have recently experienced some form of major discrimination, with religious and racial minorities and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples facing the highest levels of prejudice.

Net Zero Momentum Tracker – Banking Sector Report

Australian banks are taking steps towards net zero emissions but actions are not yet comprehensive. Prepared by ClimateWorks and MSDI, this report is part of a series focusing on major sectors of the Australian Economy and part of our Net Zero Momentum Tracker initiative. The report found that 85 percent of banks assessed are taking steps to reduce their investment and lending portfolio emissions. But it also found that these commitments are not yet comprehensive or fully aligned with the Paris Climate Agreement goal of net zero emissions before 2050.

Net Zero Momentum Tracker – Property Sector Report

Australia’s property sector is building momentum towards net zero emissions. Sector-wide adoption of a net zero ambition is critical to achieving Paris-aligned targets. This report is part of a series focusing on major sectors of the Australian economy as part of our Net Zero Momentum Tracker initiative. Examining 21 companies within Australia’s property sector, it found that almost half of Australia’s largest listed property companies have made commitments to reduce greenhouse gas emissions that closely align with the Paris Climate Agreement.

Natural Capital Roadmap

The Natural Capital Roadmap, produced by our ClimateWorks Australia team, pulls together the ideas of over 300 leaders in farming, forestry, natural resource management, conservation, finance, policy, research and government on how to progress the natural capital agenda in Australia. Mainstream economic management has developed mature systems for measuring and valuing human forms of capital. Natural capital thinking envisions a world where equivalent systems are in place to enable more informed decisions about the environment. The roadmap was developed in collaboration with partners from the Queensland Government and NAB, with significant contributions from workshop and summit attendees.

Australian National Outlook

The year is 2060. What does Australia look like? Have we harnessed social, economic and environmental opportunities? Or are we in a slow decline? This report, produced by the CSIRO in partnership with the NAB, MSDI’s ClimateWorks Australia team, and more than 20 other organisations, identifies the challenges and opportunities we face as a nation. It signals that Australia faces a slow decline if it takes no action on the most significant economic, social and environmental challenges. If these challenges are tackled head on, Australia can look forward to a positive outlook vision, which could mean higher GDP per capita, ‘net zero’ greenhouse gas emissions by 2050, strong economic growth and energy affordability, and more liveable major cities.

Electric Vehicle Ready Government Fleets: Increasing Update and Ambition

Transport is one of the fastest growing sources of emissions in Australia. Electric vehicles fuelled by renewable energy can drastically reduce the sector’s emissions. Globally, the transition to electric vehicles has momentum, but Australia still trails behind. Local Government fleets could help speed up the transition. Based on findings from the project, our ClimateWorks Australia team produced a report in 2019 recommending three priority areas of focus: a national capacity building program to provide information and tools need for transition; events to create momentum at the executive level in Councils; and actions to reduce the capital cost barrier to transition.
