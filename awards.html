2019 Awards

We stand at the cusp of a transformational decade. These awards recognise the excellence of the people who have got us this far and the promise of what we can build when we collaborate and come together.

Monash wins Banksia Award for second year in a row

MSDI together with our partners from the Monash Science, Arts, and Business Faculties won a Banksia Award for Research and Education in 2019. The award follows MSDI’s win in the same category in 2018. The 2019 prize was awarded for Monash’s Master of Environment and Sustainability. The team was recognised for its new approach to interdisciplinary education and collaboration.

MSDI researcher named Victoria’s Young Water Professional of the Year

MSDI’s Dr Paul Satur was recognised in 2019 for his extraordinary contribution to the water sector in Australia. An environmental and social science researcher still in the early stages of his career, Paul was named Victoria’s Young Water Professional of the Year for his substantial contribution to the Australian water sector in just ten years. It acknowledged his deep passion for developing the knowledge, tools and influence to foster a socially inclusive and sustainable water future for everyone. In 2020, Paul was named the Young Water Professional of the Year in the Australian Water Awards.

Professor Rob Skinner awarded Queen’s Birthday Honour

MSDI’s Director of Monash Water Sensitive Cities was named a Member (AM) of the Order of Australia in the 2019 Queen’s Birthday Honours in recognition of his significant service to environmental water management during a career that has earned him a reputation as one of Australia’s great champions of water sensitive urban design.

MSDI’s teachers awarded for their excellence

MSDI’s education team received not one, but two, awards in the Monash Vice Chancellor’s Education and Research Awards. Lecturer and Senior Learning Coordinator, Gitanjali Bedi, won a citation for Outstanding Contribution to Student Learning for the undergraduate capstone unit – Sustainability Practice and Organisations. MSDI’s Deputy Director of Education, Associate Professor Annette Bos, and Lecturer David Robertson, also won a team award for Teaching Excellence for the contributions to the Monash cross-faculty Master of Environment and Sustainability. The awards are the top level of recognition available for education at Monash.

Monash Students win MSDI scholarship to New York

Four Monash students won highly competitive MSDI scholarships to attend the prestigious International Conference on Sustainable Development in New York in September 2019. The international conference brings together students from the world’s top universities and leaders in academia, public and private sectors and civil society to discuss ways of moving forward to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals.

MSDI Business Student Awarded 2019 Flourish Prize

MSDI alumni and Monash Business student Vladimir Slaviero was awarded a 2019 Flourish Prize for his story Tiny Ideas, Massive Impact, about innovative Australian business, Fred’s Tiny Houses. The piece was written under the direction of MSDI Lecturer, Gitanjali Bedi and awarded for SDG 11. The Flourish Prize awards stories about positive, global business innovations that can help achieve the Sustainable Development Goals.

MSDI Research Assistant Wins Westpac Future Leaders Scholarship

MSDI Research Assistant, Jarrod Grainger-Brown, was awarded a prestigious Westpac Future Leaders Scholarship in 2019. The Scholarship is awarded to outstanding postgraduate students who are investigating solutions to some of Australia’s most complex issues. Through the scholarship, Jarrod is undertaking his PhD with Monash Sustainable Development Institute to investigate how the Sustainable Development Goals can be integrated into urban and city planning.
