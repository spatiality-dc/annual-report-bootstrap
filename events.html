2019 Events

Our work takes us around the country, region and the world. In 2019, we were privileged to work with and hear from international thought-leaders and change-makers, and to present at some of the world’s top sustainable development events.

International Events

Pacific Urban Forum, Fiji

MSDI, through the RISE program, was a co-sponsor for this year's Pacific Urban Forum, held in Nadi, Fiji in July 2019. Held every four years, the Pacific Urban Forum is the premier regional event bringing together government representatives and urban development stakeholders to engage in policy dialogue towards a sustainable urban future for the Pacific. Over 25 countries participated in the 2019 event with the theme ‘Accelerating implementation of the New Urban Agenda to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals in the Pacific’.

MSDI’s Matthew French, RISE Program Manager, and Amalie Wright from RISE's Design and Engagement team showcased how RISE places evidence at the heart of a revitalisation program's efforts, discussing why better evidence is needed of what works in terms of delivering sustainable, cost-effective health and environmental improvements. MSDI’s Julie Boulton was the focal point for the academia stakeholder group at the Forum. The closing of the forum saw Pacific Island Countries come together to endorse a collective Declaration, outlining the direction and actions that their governments will take to ensure that Pacific urban areas are ‘prosperous, inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable’.

International Conference on Sustainable Development and Climate Week, New York City

Six MSDI staff and eight Monash students converged on New York in late September to participate in a number of events including the International Conference on Sustainable Development (ICSD), the Sustainable Development Solutions Network (SDSN) meetings, Climate Week, and to promote the new MSDI/McMaster Social Systems Evidence initiative (SSE). This year’s activities in New York also included the first UN SDG Summit to review progress on the SDGs since they were adopted in 2015.

Two MSDI staff and two student delegates presented papers, and six students presented posters as part of the ICSD. In her role as SDSN Network Manager, MSDI’s Dr Tahl Kestin co-chaired two sessions at the conference on Transforming the Higher Education System to Accelerate SDG Implementation. MSDI’s Chair, Professor John Thwaites, hosted a panel session on the challenges of translating research evidence into public policy and strategies to address barriers to translation around the world.

SDSN AusNZPacific and SDSN Canada co-hosted a workshop to introduce the Social Systems Evidence platform to key international stakeholders and seek feedback on how we could best connect the platform to policymakers and others vested in advancing the SDGs. The team also presented to the SDSN Leadership Council as part of a session examining the role of data and evidence in achieving the SDGs. Anna Skarbek, CEO of MSDI’s ClimateWorks Australia team, attended a number of events as part of Climate Week including a World Green Building Council event where the ClimateWorks Net Zero Momentum Tracker’s property report was presented.

The Monash Alumni Office in NY hosted a reception in honour of MSDI, held at and with the kind support of the National Australia Bank’s New York Office. This is the fourth year this event has been held and this year’s guest speaker was Monash alumna Mina Guli, a global leader, entrepreneur and adventurer.

COP25, Madrid

Monash University became an official Observer Organisation of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change in 2016 and has since sent annual delegations to their Conference of Parties (COPs).

In 2019, a delegation of 16 staff and students attended the 2019 United Nations Climate Change Conference (COP25) in Madrid in December. The delegation, which included specialists in environmental law, energy transitions and climate change communication, came from several of the university's leading centres and institutes including the Monash Energy Institute, the Monash Climate Change Communication Research Hub and MSDI.

Monash delegates were invited to speak at a wide range of events including press conferences, panel discussions and side events.

MSDI’s ClimateWorks’ team spoke at SDSN’s Low Emissions Solutions Conference with Professor Jeffrey Sachs where the importance of Southeast Asian countries joining the green race was discussed, and ClimateWorks research on how Southeast Asian countries can build a comparative advantage in low carbon technologies was presented.

The team also participated in official side events with the UNFCCC Secretariat. A global group of university students, including Monash student delegates, partnered together to facilitate over 10 side events and press conferences on student research linked to the SDGs. Other events included "Enhancing Ambition through International Instruments & Innovations from the Paris Agreement & Beyond" and “A Roadmap on the future: Identifying next steps and integrating practical approaches to good governance in implementing the Katowice Climate Package”.

Monash students also met with Australian government representatives, Global Voices, and youth from across Australia (including secondary school students), to give a student perspective on youth representation at COP and recommend ways in which the government could engage with youth delegates at COP26.

Monash’s involvement and visibility at the event was maximised through an excellent collaboration between MSDI and Monash Global Engagement who coordinated the delegation.

Seminars and Public Events

Research Seminar Series

In 2019 MSDI launched its Research Seminar Series to foster interdisciplinary conversations between academics on the grand sustainable development challenges of our time. This lecture series features academics from across Monash and around the world and aims to spark dialogue and collaboration across the Monash research community for a more sustainable future.

16 Research Seminars were held in 2019 and included topics such as Nature’s New Urban Politics, Designing Safer Cities for Women and Girls, Making Behaviour Change Research Useful, The Sharing Economy, Sustainable Futures for Industrial Lands, Sustainable Business Models, Frameworks and Evidence for Accelerating Energy and Low Carbon Transitions, and Illicit Financial Flow.

The Change Room

MSDI’s BehaviourWorks team delivered four live Change Room events in 2019. Hosted by Geoff Paine and academics from MSDI’s BehaviourWorks team, The Change Room brings research and entertainment together to help us understand behavioural science in a fun, interactive way. Events in 2019 explored topics such as ‘The Wheel of Bias’ with Dr Sarah Kneebone, ‘Mind Games’ (behavioural experiments) with Dr Fraser Tull, ‘The Dark Side’ (of manipulation for gain) with Abby Wild, and ‘Culture’ with Dr Morgan Tear.

Sustainable Finance with Dr Ben Caldecott

In March, ClimateWorks Australia hosted Dr Ben Caldecott, Founding Director of the Oxford Sustainable Finance Programme at the University of Oxford. In his conversation with ClimateWorks Australia’s CEO Anna Skarbek, Dr Caldecott discussed the role of finance in limiting climate change to well below 2 degrees, the challenges of climate change for investors, and the financial system more broadly, including the potential for significant asset stranding.

Dr Caldecott’s work in developing new tools to support investor influence on companies and their environmental footprints was also explored and drew on his publications on Carbon Lock-In Curves and on the findings of the 7th Sustainable Finance Forum.

Learn more about this event.

Green Chemistry with Professor John Warner

In August, MSDI hosted a seminar by Professor John Warner, President and Chief Technology Officer at the Warner Babcock Institute and Adjunct Professor in the School of Chemistry at Monash. Recently named one of ‘25 Visionaries Changing the World’, Professor Warner is the recipient of the 2014 Perkin Medal - the highest honour in American industrial chemistry. He is best known as one of the founders of the field of Green Chemistry - a form of chemistry which seeks to design materials and products that limit the impact on human health and the environment while still being economically competitive.

In his lecture, John presented the history of Green Chemistry, challenged the fundamentals of how chemistry is being taught and encouraged us all to think about how products are made to create a safer and more sustainable future.

Law Needs Feminism Because…

In September, Associate Professor Becky Batagol teamed up with Monash Law student Maria Pirozek to bring the hugely successful "Law Needs Feminism Because" project to Australia to put a spotlight on gender inequality in the legal profession.

The Australian exhibition, which ran until December, helped shine a light on Sustainable Development Goals 5 (Gender Equality) and 10 (Reduced Inequalities) and was held at Monash University's Law Library at Clayton. Originally a Canadian-based photography project, “Law Needs Feminism Because” had spread across 18 Canadian universities but the Australian exhibition marked the first time the project had been launched outside of North America.

Partnership and Multi-Stakeholder Events

From Townsville to Tuvalu: Health and Climate Change in Australia and the Asia Pacific Region – Report Launch

The landmark report “From Townsville to Tuvalu - Health and Climate Change in Australia and the Asia Pacific Region” produced by The Global Health Alliance with MSDI and the Monash School of Public Health and Preventative Medicine was launched in Melbourne in July 2019 with high level stakeholders in attendance and the event itself reaching room capacity.

The latest science tells us that climate change will have a big impact on human health – from childhood obesity, heart failure and diabetes, to stunting, increases in allergies, asthma attacks and waterborne diseases. The launch event brought together experts from Monash and the Doherty Institute for Infection and Immunity to discuss those impacts and explore how we might reduce them in both Australia and the Indo-Pacific region.

Read more about this report.

Today’s Problems, Yesterday’s Toolkit – Report Launch

Today’s Problems, Yesterday’s Toolkit, a joint research report by ANZSOG, The GovLab and MSDI, was officially launched at Old Parliament House, Canberra, in August 2019. Professors Rod Glover (MSDI) and Beth Noveck (GovLab), presented findings of their report to policymakers and stakeholders, and spoke of the decline in public trust in governments and the need to develop new ways of working. They also called for the development of a 21st century toolkit for public problem solving, designing more effective skills training and encouraging institutional experimentation.

Moderated by ANZSOG’s Dr Subho Banerjee, a panel discussion featuring the authors along with Sarah Pearson, Chief Innovation Officer – DFAT explored practical ways of implementing the report recommendations and fostering public entrepreneurs.

Learn more about this report.

Families Together, Oxfam Monash Partnership

Monash Arts researchers partnered with Oxfam Australia to examine the social impact of family separation on refugee settlement in Australia. Supported by MSDI's Oxfam-Monash Partnership, the Families Together report was launched in Melbourne in August along with the central report for the Oxfam Australia campaign, Stronger Together.

This collaboration between the Monash Migration and Inclusion Centre and Oxfam featured together with economic data from Deloitte Access Economics is providing evidence-based recommendations to inform policy on humanitarian family reunions in Australia.

Inclusive Australia: Social Inclusion Index Report Launch

The Inclusive Australia Social Inclusion Index, a report developed by MSDI’s BehaviourWorks Australia team, was launched in December to support and coincide with the launch of the organisation Inclusive Australia (IA), which seeks to reduce the negative impacts of exclusion. The official launch event was held in Canberra with additional events held in Melbourne and Sydney.

The Melbourne event, MC'd by Helen Kapalos - Journalist and TV presenter, was held at MSDI’s city hub with the event reaching capacity and fostering excellent dialogue between stakeholders. The keynote address at the Melbourne event was delivered by Professor Sue Elliot, Deputy Vice-Chancellor at Monash University. Professor Elliot emphasised that if we want to tackle social inclusion, it is vital that stakeholders have the tools they need to make change happen and to improve how Australians view and appreciate the differences in others.
